Giraffe

A giraffe’s eyes are the size of golf balls. Source

  • Scientic name: Giraffidae
  • Height female: up to 14 feet (4.3 m)
  • Height male: up to 18 feet (5.5 m)
  • Weight female: up to 1,500 lb (680 kg)
  • Weight male: up to 3,000 lb (1,360 kg)
  • Average lifespan: 15 to 20 years
  • Habitat: savannahs and woodlands.

The giraffe (Giraffa) is a genus of African even-toed ungulate mammals, the tallest living terrestrial animals and the largest ruminants.

A giraffe's feet are the size of a dinner plate—12 inches across (30.5 centimeters).

The recorded running speed of a giraffe is 34.7 miles per hour (56 kilometers per hour). A giraffe’s eyes are the size of golf balls.
